About Me

Hello, my name is Stanislav Cucer and I'm a beginner Web Developer.

I love code. I dream of code. I enjoy code. I find writing high quality code deeply satisfying.

Enthusiasts code out of love. If you're an enthusiast you'd write software just for fun, but one day you discovered your hobby could also be your career, and now you get paid to do what you love.

Pragmatists may enjoy coding, but they do it for the outcomes. If you're a pragmatist, you write software because it's a good career, or for what it enables you to do and build.

There's nothing inherently good or bad about either, and this is just a simplification. But understanding your own starting point can help you understand and avoid some of the problems you might encounter in your career.

The enthusiast developer derives significant satisfaction from their discovery and application of new solutions. They usually are obsessive tinkers who will explore and experiment with varying technologies.

Some have a deeply anchored sense of pride and authority in their knowledge base. This means they can be voracious readers and researchers. They are inquisitive and are intellectually curious, eager for knowledge, and likes to ask questions. Not like others, often boring low-level details intrigue them.

The passionate developer commits an excessive amount of time on resources and technical sites. They prefer to spend their time on technically oriented social platforms, and forums connecting with other developers on their activities and experiences.

The outcome for the enthusiast developer is a feeling that is both fulfilling and empowering.
